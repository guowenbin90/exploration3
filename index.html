Research

The goal of this study is to develop a real-time location tracking protocol that will improve the healthcare delivery process by minimizing missed opportunities caused by work delays.

People

Wenbin Guo is a Ph.D. student studying industrial engineering at the University of Missouri. His research focuses on ergonomics. He implemented various eye tracking devices such as Tobii EyeX, Hololens, Eyetech and Pupil Pro to design and develop experiments associated with human performance and behavior modeling.

Resources

We provided the devices HoloLens, Mobile Eye-XG eye tracking glasses (60Hz) and Display Transmit Unit, as well as Near Field Electromagnetic Ranging Real-Time Location System.



Ergonomics Lab Bio

The Ergonomics Lab research aims to prevent work-related musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) by understanding injury mechanisms through laboratory and field studies that utilize biomechanics, neuromuscular, exposure-response, and intervention study designs and methods.
